Veg:

Vegetarian food in hotels is an important topic, especially in regions where dietary preferences are diverse. Many hotels now focus on sourcing fresh vegetables and even growing their own produce to ensure quality and cost-effectiveness. Some hotels maintain separate kitchens for vegetarian and non-vegetarian food to respect cultural and religious sentiments. Additionally, sustainability is becoming a priority, with hotels adopting organic farming and responsible sourcing to provide healthier vegetarian

Non-Veg:

Non-vegetarian food in hotels is a significant aspect of hospitality, catering to diverse dietary preferences. Many hotels offer a wide range of non-veg dishes, from local delicacies to international cuisines, ensuring quality and hygiene standards. Some hotels maintain separate kitchens for vegetarian and non-vegetarian food to respect cultural sensitivities

Starters:

Starters play a crucial role in hotel dining, setting the tone for the meal and enhancing the guest experience. Hotels often offer a variety of vegetarian and non-vegetarian appetizers, ranging from traditional Indian delicacies to international fusion dishes. Some popular starters include fried chicken momos, stuffed naan pockets, idli Manchurian, and crispy corn chaat

Desserts:

Desserts play a crucial role in hotel dining, enhancing the overall guest experience and leaving a lasting impression. Many hotels focus on signature desserts that reflect their brand identity, such as extravagant multi-layered cakes or themed high teas.
